A. Normal pregnancy

    Basic and Clinical Sciences

    Describe anatomical changes occurring during pregnancy

    Outline physiological changes occurring in cardiovascular, respiratory and haematological systems during normal pregnancy

    Describe the tests available to confirm pregnancy

    Nuchal Translucency screening and triple test as screening tests for common trisomies

    Compare CVS and amniocentesis as diagnostic tests for aneuploidy

    Patient and Doctor

    Identify symptoms and signs of early pregnancy

    Competently take a booking history

    Conduct a routine antenatal check-up.

    Correctly identify presentation and lie

    Describe methods of accurately dating a pregnancy and outline why this is important

    Competently and sensitively examine a woman at the different stages of pregnancy

    Competently measure symphysis to fundal height

    Describe indications for ultrasound scan during pregnancy

    Describe the purpose of the 18 week morphology scan

    Discuss results of scan with pregnant woman

    Population Medicine

    Outline screening relevant to women prior to conception

    Describe the role of periconceptual folate in reduction of neural tube defects

    Describe potential impact of lifestyle factors on pregnancy

    Describe routine screening during pregnancy

    Counsel pregnant woman appropriately about the range of screening tests available during pregnancy

    Discuss different models of antenatal care

    Describe the differences in pregnancy and neonatal outcomes between Indigenous and Non-indigenous Australians, and the likely impact of these outcomes on neonatal and infant health and welfare

    Personal and Professional Development

    Discuss the right of a woman to have the major role in the choice of the most appropriate model of care for her during pregnancy and birth

    Consider cultural differences in attitudes towards pregnancy and in customs related to pregnancy and childbirth

    Consider the reasons for the difference in pregnancy and neonatal outcomes for Indigenous and Non-indigenous Australians and how these might impact on perinatal care and safe discharge. Reflect on measures for reducing adverse outcomes

    Consider cultural differences between Indigenous and Non-indigenous Australians and reflect on how these may affect communication and management of pregnancy and the newborn

B. Abnormal or high risk pregnancy

    Basic and Clinical Sciences

    Outline principles of risk identification during pregnancy and be able to discuss common risk factors in indigenous and non-indigenous populations

    Know the clinical significance of infection during pregnancy, labour, birth and the postnatal period (Rubella, GBS, CMV, Hep B, Hep C, varicella, HIV)

    Describe the different mechanisms of twinning

    Describe the interaction of the medical conditions diabetes, obesity and pregnancy induced hypertension on the pregnant woman and her fetus, and on the pregnancy

    Patient and Doctor

    Accurately identify a fetus which may be small for gestation age or have restricted intra-uterine growth

    Recognise symptoms and signs suggesting infection in a pregnant woman

    Explain to a pregnant woman the increased risks of  multiple pregnancies

    Identify reasons for the need for extra care during pregnancy for women with diabetes, obesity and pregnancy induced hypertension

    Population Medicine

    Know Australian maternal mortality/morbidity rates and fetal loss statistics

    Describe screening for selected bacterial and viral infections during pregnancy (Rubella, Hep, HIV, syphllis, GBS)

    EBM: Examine the evidence for prevention of hepatitis B transmission after birth and during lactation http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16625613

    Outline the incidence of twinning

    Identify circumstances when a screening GTT is appropriate during pregnancy

    Outline the prophylaxis for iso-immunisation

    Personal and Professional Development

    Consider the effects of fetal abnormality on families

    Understand the long term implications of intrauterine growth restriction on adult health (fetal origins of adult disease)

    Discuss ethical issues involved in therapeutic termination

    EBM: Examine the evidence for prevention of rhesus iso-immunisation  http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10796089
